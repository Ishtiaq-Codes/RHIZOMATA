The Philosophy

Destiny is not given.
It is chosen.

Most games use chance — dice, card draws, random events — as the primary engine of excitement. Rhizomata proposes a different approach: all consequences are visible before every choice is made.


There are no hidden reveals, no dice, no luck in the result of any decision. The tension is not informational. It is moral. Every person is a hostage to what they have earned.

— Created by Amna Noor

The Elements

Identity Cards

In classical philosophy, your element is assigned at birth — fixed, inherited, and beyond your control. Rhizomata challenges this. The element is dealt randomly at the start of the game, but what you do with it is entirely a matter of choice. It is a starting condition, not a sentence.

Click a card to reveal its true identity.

The Dilemmas

Situation Cards

The tension between who we want to be and who we actually become in moments of pressure is the core of the game. You will face 60 unique moral decisions. Both options display their full consequences before you decide. No dice. No luck.

Click to face your fate.

The Frame

Context Cards

A moral decision never exists in a vacuum. The game features 42 unique Context Cards that interlock with the 60 Situation Cards, producing 2,520 unique pairings. A single scenario drastically shifts depending on the context drawn.

The Vessels

The Dual Self

You do not play a single piece. You control two pawns simultaneously. The Stability Pawn grounds you in safety and restraint. The Ambition Pawn drives you toward risk and glory. Your true character exists in the tension between the two.
